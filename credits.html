The genesis of what you see here was a 2003 retrospective of my work at the Addison/Ripley Gallery in Washington, DC. Christopher Addison wisely suggested interweaving images and text to create a narrative. Robin Groom, my former colleague at The Washington Post and the person who still makes the Style section run on time, was invaluable in pulling all the material together.

Kelly Niland, designer extraordinaire, created the identity for the site. The beta HTML was coded by Mike Evans. Helen Kalisher, a wonderful web designer, handled the final implementation as well as the digital conversion of the images. Black & white prints for the conversions were made by Gamma; color prints by Light Waves.
